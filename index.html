Yasukuni Sumo & East Tokyo

The highlight of day two: a rare chance to watch a free outdoor ceremonial sumo tournament at Yasukuni Shrine — the Spring Jungyo Honozumo. Combine it with Senso-ji and the Asakusa neighbourhood for a packed but unforgettable day.

  • 07:30 — Leave Asakusa early; take the Ginza Line to Kudanshita (~20 min, direct). Aim to arrive at Yasukuni Shrine by 08:00 to queue for a good spot
  • 08:30 — Gates open at the shrine's sumo ring (Jungyo schedule). Free entry, first-come first-served — 6,000 capacity
  • 09:00 — Dohyo-iri (ring ceremony) begins; lower-ranked bouts and special performances throughout the morning — sumo comedy skits (shokkiri), sumo folk songs, and taiko drumming. Grab snacks from vendors inside the grounds
  • 13:30 — Yokozuna & top-ranked wrestlers' shrine worship parade — the ceremonial highlight, wrestlers in full kesho-mawashi (decorative aprons)
  • 14:00 — Makuuchi (top division) ring-entering ceremony & bouts — the main event; all the top wrestlers compete
  • ~15:00 — Event finishes. Walk through the shrine grounds and head out
  • 15:30 — Metro back to Asakusa (~20 min). Visit Senso-ji Temple & Nakamise shopping street: the iconic thunder gate, incense hall, and the full approach lined with street food and souvenir stalls — less crowded in the late afternoon on a Monday
  • 17:00 — Wander the backstreets west of Senso-ji: small neighbourhood shrines, traditional sweet shops (try ningyo-yaki), and old craft workshops the tourists walk right past
  • 18:30 — Dinner on Hoppy Street: Asakusa's lively izakaya strip with outdoor seating, cheap drinks, and yakitori — perfect for the group's first proper night out together
💡 Sumo tips: Arrive by 08:00 — the shrine fills up and entry may be restricted once 6,000 people are inside. Bring a small cushion or towel to sit on (ground seating). No flash photography near the ring. The event runs rain or shine (light rain only — cancelled in heavy rain). You can leave and re-enter before 14:40, after which no re-entry is allowed. Use public transport — no parking available. Full event info (Japanese)

🗓️ More sumo nearby: The Spring Jungyo also visits Fuchu (Apr 23) — 30 min from Shinjuku — if you want a second dose during your final Tokyo days.

Fushimi Inari, Nishiki Market & Arashiyama
  • 08:00 — Fushimi Inari Taisha: the famous thousands of vermilion torii gates; hike as far up the mountain as energy allows — the full loop takes 2.5–3 hours, but you can turn back at any point. Best experienced early before the crowds arrive
  • 11:00 — Sanjusangendo: breathtaking hall of 1,001 life-size golden Kannon statues — one of the most visually stunning rooms in all of Japan (30–40 min). Just 10 min from Fushimi Inari by train
  • 12:00 — Nishiki Market: Kyoto's famous covered food market — graze on street snacks, pickles, fresh tofu, and local specialties while walking the narrow lane. Perfect for lunch; stalls are all open midday
  • 14:00 — Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion): iconic gold-leaf temple reflected in a tranquil pond
  • 16:00 — Arashiyama Bamboo Grove: the late afternoon light filtering through the bamboo is beautiful; walk to Tenryu-ji garden and the Arashiyama Park viewpoint along the river. Optional: continue 20 min north to Otagi Nenbutsu-ji — a hidden temple with 1,200+ whimsical stone statues each with unique expressions (closes 17:00, get there by 16:30)
  • 18:00 — Dinner at a riverside Arashiyama restaurant, or head back to central Kyoto (try kaiseki or yudofu tofu cuisine)

🦌 Day Trip to Nara & Uji
  • 09:00 — Depart Kyoto Station on JR Nara Line (~45 min, ¥720)
  • 10:00 — Nara Park: meet the famous free-roaming sika deer; buy shika senbei (deer crackers)

  • 07:30 — Depart Osaka (Shin-Osaka) on Shinkansen to Hiroshima (~1 hr 20 min)

Transport Essentials

  • IC Card (Suica/Pasmo): buy at Haneda/Narita on arrival; tap-in everywhere for local trains, buses, even convenience stores. Load via Apple Wallet — no physical card needed
  • Narita ↔ Tokyo: take the Keisei Skyliner (¥2,570) — faster and cheaper than N'EX; runs to Ueno then easy onward to Asakusa/Ikebukuro
  • Kansai Airport → Kyoto: Haruka Express (~¥3,490); book point-to-point tickets at the airport
  • Kyoto → Osaka: take the Hankyu Line from Kyoto-Kawaramachi (¥410) — no booking needed, very frequent
  • Osaka → Kansai Airport: Nankai Line from Namba (~¥920, ~40 min) — simple and cheap
  • Osaka ↔ Hiroshima Shinkansen: buy individual tickets (¥10,520 each way); book in advance for 6 seats together
  • Shinjuku → Kawaguchiko bus: ~¥2,200 each way (~1.5–2 hrs); book via Highway Bus Online or Willer Express — popular route, reserve early for 6
  • Taxis: download the Go app (Japan's Uber equivalent) — essential for groups of 6 (need 2 taxis). Also useful for the late-night Narita arrival on Apr 22

Money & Practical Tips

  • Japan is still largely cash-based — carry yen; 7-Eleven ATMs accept foreign cards
  • Get a pocket WiFi or eSIM for all 6 people; essential for navigation
  • Restaurant reservations: use Tableall or Pocket Concierge apps for high-end spots
  • Google Maps or HyperDia app for train navigation
  • Convenience stores (konbini) are genuinely amazing — breakfast, lunch, snacks sorted
  • Bring an adapter (Japan uses Type A / 100V plugs)

Group of 6 — Special Notes

  • Reserve group seating on all long-distance trains — Shinkansen has 3+3 layout, fits groups well
  • Many taxis fit 4; for 6 people, use two taxis or Go app (Japanese Uber equivalent)
  • Some restaurants have min. group size limits — book ahead to confirm
  • Luggage forwarding (takuhaibin): send bags between Airbnbs for ~¥2,000–3,000/bag — takes 1 day; saves dragging luggage on transit days (Apr 17 Kyoto→Osaka especially). Ask your host the evening before
  • Shibuya Sky (Apr 23): book the ~17:00 premium slot with 2 drinks; sunset is ~18:17 — arrive early to secure a good spot on the open-air rooftop
  • April is Golden Week end — April 29+ gets very busy. You depart April 26/27, so mostly fine, but expect bigger crowds from April 25
  • April = late cherry blossoms. Ueno, Maruyama Park (Kyoto), and Osaka Castle Park may still have blossoms early in the trip!
