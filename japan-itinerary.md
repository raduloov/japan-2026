### Apr 13 (Mon) — Yasukuni Sumo & East Tokyo

The highlight of day two: a rare chance to watch a **free outdoor ceremonial sumo tournament** at Yasukuni Shrine — the Spring Jungyo Honozumo. Combine it with Senso-ji and the Asakusa neighbourhood for a packed but unforgettable day.

- [ ] **07:30** — Leave Asakusa early; take the **Ginza Line to Kudanshita** (~20 min, direct). Aim to arrive at [Yasukuni Shrine](https://www.yasukuni.or.jp/) by 08:00 to queue for a good spot
- [ ] **08:30** — **Gates open** at the shrine's sumo ring ([Jungyo schedule](https://www.sumo.or.jp/EnJungyo/schedule/)). Free entry, first-come first-served — 6,000 capacity
- [ ] **09:00** — **Dohyo-iri (ring ceremony)** begins; lower-ranked bouts and special performances throughout the morning — sumo comedy skits (初切 *shokkiri*), sumo folk songs, and taiko drumming. Grab snacks from vendors inside the grounds
- [ ] **13:30** — **Yokozuna & top-ranked wrestlers' shrine worship parade** — the ceremonial highlight, wrestlers in full kesho-mawashi (decorative aprons)
- [ ] **14:00** — **Makuuchi (top division) ring-entering ceremony & bouts** — the main event; all the top wrestlers compete
- [ ] **~15:00** — Event finishes. Walk through the shrine grounds and head out
- [ ] **15:30** — Metro back to Asakusa (~20 min). Visit [**Senso-ji Temple & Nakamise shopping street**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Sensoji+Temple+Tokyo): the iconic thunder gate, incense hall, and the full approach lined with street food and souvenir stalls — less crowded in the late afternoon on a Monday
- [ ] **17:00** — Wander the [**backstreets west of Senso-ji**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Asakusa+backstreets+west+Sensoji+Tokyo): small neighbourhood shrines, traditional sweet shops (try ningyo-yaki), and old craft workshops the tourists walk right past
- [ ] **18:30** — Dinner on [**Hoppy Street**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hoppy+Street+Asakusa+Tokyo): Asakusa's lively izakaya strip with outdoor seating, cheap drinks, and yakitori — perfect for the group's first proper night out together

> 💡 **Sumo tips**: Arrive by 08:00 — the shrine fills up and entry may be restricted once 6,000 people are inside. Bring a small cushion or towel to sit on (ground seating). No flash photography near the ring. The event runs rain or shine (light rain only — cancelled in heavy rain). You can leave and re-enter before 14:40, after which no re-entry is allowed. Use public transport — no parking available. [Full event info (Japanese)](https://www.yasukuni.or.jp/news_detail.html?id=676)
>
> 🗓️ **More sumo nearby**: The Spring Jungyo also visits [Fuchu (Apr 23)](https://www.sumo.or.jp/EnJungyo/schedule/) — 30 min from Shinjuku — if you want a second dose during your final Tokyo days.

## Phase 02 — Kyoto & Nara

京都 · 14 April — 17 April · 3 nights · [**Airbnb**](https://www.airbnb.co.uk/trips/v1/reservation-details/ro/RESERVATION2_CHECKIN/HM28C25FP2)

### Apr 15 (Wed) — Fushimi Inari, Nishiki Market & Arashiyama

- [ ] **08:00** — [**Fushimi Inari Taisha**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Fushimi+Inari+Taisha+Kyoto): the famous thousands of vermilion torii gates; hike as far up the mountain as energy allows — the full loop takes 2.5–3 hours, but you can turn back at any point. Best experienced early before the crowds arrive
- [ ] **11:00** — [**Sanjusangendo**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Sanjusangendo+Kyoto): breathtaking hall of 1,001 life-size golden Kannon statues — one of the most visually stunning rooms in all of Japan (30–40 min). Just 10 min from Fushimi Inari by train
- [ ] **12:00** — [**Nishiki Market**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Nishiki+Market+Kyoto): Kyoto's famous covered food market — graze on street snacks, pickles, fresh tofu, and local specialties while walking the narrow lane. Perfect for lunch; stalls are all open midday
- [ ] **14:00** — [**Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Kinkakuji+Temple+Kyoto): iconic gold-leaf temple reflected in a tranquil pond
- [ ] **16:00** — [**Arashiyama Bamboo Grove**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Arashiyama+Bamboo+Grove+Kyoto): the late afternoon light filtering through the bamboo is beautiful; walk to [**Tenryu-ji garden**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Tenryuji+Temple+Kyoto) and the [**Arashiyama Park viewpoint**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Arashiyama+Park+Kyoto) along the river. **Optional**: continue 20 min north to [**Otagi Nenbutsu-ji**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Otagi+Nenbutsuji+Temple+Kyoto) — a hidden temple with 1,200+ whimsical stone statues each with unique expressions (closes 17:00, get there by 16:30)
- [ ] **18:00** — Dinner at a riverside Arashiyama restaurant, or head back to central Kyoto (try kaiseki or yudofu tofu cuisine)

### Apr 16 (Thu) — 🦌 Day Trip to Nara & Uji `DAY TRIP`

- [ ] **09:00** — Depart Kyoto Station on JR Nara Line (~45 min, ¥720)
- [ ] **10:00** — [**Nara Park**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Nara+Park): meet the famous **free-roaming sika deer**; buy shika senbei (deer crackers)
- [ ] **11:00** — [**Todai-ji Temple**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Todaiji+Temple+Nara): the massive bronze **Great Buddha (Daibutsu)**, one of Japan's most iconic sights; try squeezing through the wooden pillar hole!
- [ ] **12:30** — Lunch in Nara; try local kakinoha-zushi or soba
- [ ] **14:00** — [**Kasuga Taisha**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Kasuga+Taisha+Nara): atmospheric Shinto shrine with hundreds of stone lanterns in a forest setting
- [ ] **14:45** — [**Nakatanidou**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Nakatanidou+Nara): famous shop where they pound fresh mochi by hand at lightning speed right in front of you — one of Nara's most fun street food moments
- [ ] **15:15** — [**Naramachi**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Naramachi+Nara): historic merchant district; browse craft shops and grab matcha sweets
- [ ] **16:00** — Train to [**Uji**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Uji+Kyoto+Japan) (~45–50 min from Nara via transfer): famous for green tea and the stunning [**Byodo-in Temple**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Byodoin+Temple+Uji) (on the ¥10 coin); grab a matcha soft serve on the way
- [ ] **18:00** — Return to Kyoto for dinner (~20 min from Uji)

> 💡 Nara is very walkable — everything is within ~2 km. Great for the whole group. Book Todai-ji tickets online to skip lines. Note: Nara → Uji requires a transfer (no direct train) so allow ~45–50 min, not 30.

| Kyoto → Nara         | Nara → Uji                 | Uji → Kyoto |
| -------------------- | -------------------------- | ----------- |
| JR Nara Line ~45 min | ~45–50 min (with transfer) | ~20 min     |

### Apr 17 (Fri) — Kiyomizu-dera, Nijo Castle & More Kyoto → Osaka `TRANSIT`

- [ ] **07:00** — [**Kiyomizu-dera Temple**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Kiyomizudera+Temple+Kyoto): one of Kyoto's most iconic temples on a wooden hillside platform; magical at dawn before the crowds arrive (opens 06:00)
- [ ] **08:00** — Walk [**Sannenzaka & Ninenzaka**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Sannenzaka+Ninenzaka+Kyoto): beautiful cobblestone streets winding down from the temple; souvenir shops, matcha cafés, traditional townhouses
- [ ] **09:30** — [**Nijo Castle**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Nijo+Castle+Kyoto): shogun's Kyoto residence; famous "nightingale floors" that squeak when walked on
- [ ] **11:00** — Free time in Kyoto; options: [**Kyoto Tower**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Kyoto+Tower) observation deck (right at the station, quick visit), [**Philosopher's Path**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Philosophers+Path+Kyoto) cherry blossom walk, [**Pontocho Alley**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Pontocho+Alley+Kyoto) lunch by the river, or revisit a favourite spot
- [ ] **13:00** — 🍵 **Maiko tea ceremony in Gion** — private group experience with a maiko (apprentice geisha): traditional tea service, dance performance, conversation and photos (~1 hr). Book [**Maikoya Kyoto**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Maikoya+Kyoto) or [**Gion Hatanaka**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Gion+Hatanaka+Kyoto) for 6 in advance
- [ ] **15:00** — Take the **Hankyu Line** to Osaka (~45 min, very affordable)
- [ ] Check in to [**Osaka Airbnb**](https://www.airbnb.co.uk/trips/v1/reservation-details/ro/RESERVATION2_CHECKIN/HMCCECAJP5) in [**Namba / Shinsaibashi**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Namba+Shinsaibashi+Osaka) area
- [ ] **19:00** — [**Dotonbori**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Dotonbori+Osaka): Osaka's electric food & entertainment canal; try **takoyaki** (octopus balls), **okonomiyaki**, and **kushikatsu**

> 💡 **Luggage tip**: Use **takuhaibin** (luggage forwarding) — send your bags from your **Kyoto Airbnb** to your **Osaka Airbnb** the evening before for ~¥2,000/bag. Ask the host on Apr 16. This way you sightsee all morning unburdened and just take the train to Osaka with a day bag.

| Kyoto → Osaka       | Cost          |
| ------------------- | ------------- |
| Hankyu Line ~45 min | ¥410 each way |

## Phase 03 — Osaka & Hiroshima

大阪 · 17 April — 22 April · 5 nights · [**Airbnb**](https://www.airbnb.co.uk/trips/v1/reservation-details/ro/RESERVATION2_CHECKIN/HMCCECAJP5)

### Apr 18 (Sat) — Osaka Castle & Shinsekai

- [ ] **09:00** — [**Osaka Castle**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Osaka+Castle): the castle grounds, museum inside the keep, and the lovely park surrounding it
- [ ] **12:00** — [**Kuromon Ichiba Market**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Kuromon+Ichiba+Market+Osaka): Osaka's "Kitchen" — fresh seafood, wagyu skewers, and local snacks
- [ ] **14:00** — [**Shinsekai district**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Shinsekai+Osaka): retro 1950s-aesthetic neighborhood; try kushikatsu (deep fried skewers — the Osaka invention)
- [ ] **18:00** — [**Umeda Sky Building**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Umeda+Sky+Building+Osaka): spectacular open-air observation platform atop two linked towers; stay for sunset

### Apr 19 (Sun) — 🕊️ Day Trip to Hiroshima & Miyajima `DAY TRIP`

- [ ] **07:30** — Depart Osaka ([Shin-Osaka](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Shin-Osaka+Station)) on **Shinkansen** to Hiroshima (~1 hr 20 min)
- [ ] **09:00–12:00** — [**Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park & Museum**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hiroshima+Peace+Memorial+Park): deeply moving; the [A-Bomb Dome](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Atomic+Bomb+Dome+Hiroshima), the Children's Peace Monument; allow 2–3 hours
- [ ] **12:30** — Lunch in Hiroshima; try Hiroshima-style **okonomiyaki** and **oysters** (Hiroshima is Japan's oyster capital)
- [ ] **14:00** — Head to [**Miyajima Island**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Miyajima+Island+Hiroshima) (Itsukushima): allow 30–40 min to reach the ferry terminal from the city centre, plus ~10 min ferry ride. Realistic arrival on the island ~14:45–15:00
- [ ] Explore the [**floating torii gate**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Itsukushima+Shrine+Torii+Gate+Miyajima) of Itsukushima Shrine; roam with free deer; enjoy the island at a relaxed pace
- [ ] **Optional**: [**Mt. Misen**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Mt+Misen+Miyajima) via ropeway + short hike to the summit (535m) — panoramic views over the Seto Inland Sea; allow ~2 hrs round trip with ropeway, longer if hiking both ways. Only feasible if you arrive on the island by 14:30 and skip some shrine time
- [ ] **18:00** — Ferry back; **Shinkansen back to Osaka**, arrive ~20:00–20:30

> ⚠️ This is a full, emotionally significant day. The Peace Museum is essential but heavy — take breaks. The Mt. Misen ropeway + summit adds ~2 hrs — decide on the island based on energy and time. The shrine area and torii gate alone are well worth the trip without it. Book Shinkansen in advance for 6 people.

## Phase 04 — Ikebukuro & Tokyo

東京 · 池袋 · 22 April — 27 April · 5 nights · [**Airbnb**](https://www.airbnb.co.uk/trips/v1/reservation-details/ro/RESERVATION2_CHECKIN/HMC8K4FM5Y)

### Apr 23 (Thu) — Shinjuku, Meiji, Harajuku, teamLab & Shibuya Sky Sunset

- [ ] **09:00** — [**Shinjuku Gyoen**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Shinjuku+Gyoen+National+Garden): one of Tokyo's most beautiful gardens — late cherry blossoms possible mid-April; peaceful and spacious (opens 9:00)
- [ ] **10:30** — [**Meiji Jingu Shrine**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Meiji+Jingu+Shrine+Tokyo): tranquil forested grounds in the heart of the city
- [ ] **12:00** — [**Harajuku & Omotesando**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Harajuku+Omotesando+Tokyo): [Takeshita Street](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Takeshita+Street+Harajuku+Tokyo), the most architecturally impressive shopping boulevard in Japan; lunch here
- [ ] **14:00** — [**teamLab Planets Toyosu**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=teamLab+Planets+Toyosu+Tokyo): immersive digital art — walk barefoot through water and interactive light installations (~2 hrs; book timed entry in advance). ⚠️ Aim to leave by **16:00 sharp** — transit to Shibuya takes ~30 min
- [ ] **16:30** — Head to Shibuya; quick stop at [**Hachiko statue**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hachiko+Statue+Shibuya+Tokyo)
- [ ] **17:00** — [**Shibuya Sky**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Shibuya+Sky+Tokyo) for sunset (~18:17) — book the premium slot with 2-drink option; watch the city transition from golden hour to glittering lights from the open-air rooftop
- [ ] **19:00** — Down to street level for [**Shibuya Crossing**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Shibuya+Crossing+Tokyo) at night — the neon-lit scramble is best after dark
- [ ] **19:30** — [**Udagawacho backstreets**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Udagawacho+Shibuya+Tokyo) and the enormous [**Don Quijote**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Don+Quijote+Shibuya+Tokyo) — the ultimate Japanese discount store for cosmetics, snacks, electronics and souvenirs

## Logistics

### Transport Essentials

- **IC Card** (Suica/Pasmo): buy at Haneda/Narita on arrival; tap-in everywhere for local trains, buses, even convenience stores. Load via **Apple Wallet** — no physical card needed
- **Narita ↔ Tokyo**: take the **Keisei Skyliner** (¥2,570) — faster and cheaper than N'EX; runs to Ueno then easy onward to Asakusa/Ikebukuro
- **Kansai Airport → Kyoto**: **Haruka Express** (~¥3,490); book point-to-point tickets at the airport
- **Kyoto → Osaka**: take the **Hankyu Line** from Kyoto-Kawaramachi (¥410) — no booking needed, very frequent
- **Osaka → Kansai Airport**: **Nankai Line** from Namba (~¥920, ~40 min) — simple and cheap
- **Osaka ↔ Hiroshima Shinkansen**: buy individual tickets (¥10,520 each way); book in advance for 6 seats together
- **Shinjuku → Kawaguchiko bus**: ~¥2,200 each way (~1.5–2 hrs); book via **Highway Bus Online** or **Willer Express** — popular route, reserve early for 6
- **Taxis**: download the **Go app** (Japan's Uber equivalent) — essential for groups of 6 (need 2 taxis). Also useful for the late-night Narita arrival on Apr 22

### Money & Practical Tips

- Japan is still largely **cash-based** — carry yen; 7-Eleven ATMs accept foreign cards
- Get a **pocket WiFi or eSIM** for all 6 people; essential for navigation
- Restaurant reservations: use **Tableall** or **Pocket Concierge** apps for high-end spots
- Google Maps or **HyperDia** app for train navigation
- Convenience stores (konbini) are genuinely amazing — breakfast, lunch, snacks sorted
- Bring an **adapter** (Japan uses Type A / 100V plugs)

### Group of 6 — Special Notes

- Reserve **group seating** on all long-distance trains — Shinkansen has 3+3 layout, fits groups well
- Many taxis fit 4; for 6 people, use **two taxis** or **Go app** (Japanese Uber equivalent)
- Some restaurants have **min. group size limits** — book ahead to confirm
- **Luggage forwarding** (takuhaibin): send bags between **Airbnbs** for ~¥2,000–3,000/bag — takes 1 day; saves dragging luggage on transit days (Apr 17 Kyoto→Osaka especially). Ask your host the evening before
- [**Shibuya Sky**](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Shibuya+Sky+Tokyo) (Apr 23): book the ~17:00 premium slot with 2 drinks; sunset is ~18:17 — arrive early to secure a good spot on the open-air rooftop
- April is **Golden Week** end — April 29+ gets very busy. You depart April 26/27, so mostly fine, but expect bigger crowds from April 25
- **April = late cherry blossoms**. Ueno, Maruyama Park (Kyoto), and Osaka Castle Park may still have blossoms early in the trip!
