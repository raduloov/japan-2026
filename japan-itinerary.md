### Apr 13 (Sun) — Asakusa & East Tokyo

A relaxed day staying close to base — soak in the neighbourhood, no rush, no cross-city metro marathons on day two of jet lag.

- **09:00** — **Senso-ji Temple & Nakamise shopping street**: the iconic thunder gate, incense hall, and the full approach lined with street food and souvenir stalls. Go at opening for thinner crowds, especially on a Sunday morning.
- **10:30** — Wander the **backstreets west of Senso-ji**: small neighbourhood shrines, traditional sweet shops (try ningyo-yaki), and old craft workshops the tourists walk right past
- **12:00** — Walk south to **Kuramae** (~15 min along the river): Tokyo's artisan/craft neighbourhood — small-batch coffee roasters, leather goods workshops, handmade stationery. Grab lunch here at one of the quiet cafés
- **14:00** — **Ginza Line to Omotesando** (~30 min, direct, no transfers): visit the **Apple Store Omotesando** flagship
- **15:30** — Ginza Line back to Asakusa area
- **16:00** — **Ryogoku** (one stop from Asakusa): the sumo district — Kokugikan arena, Edo-Tokyo Museum area, and the riverside. Even outside tournament season the neighbourhood has great atmosphere
- **18:30** — Dinner on **Hoppy Street**: Asakusa's lively izakaya strip with outdoor seating, cheap drinks, and yakitori — perfect for the group's first proper night out together

> 💡 Everything today is walking distance or one train stop, except the Omotesando detour which is a single direct metro line. Great pacing for jet lag.

## Phase 02 — Kyoto & Nara

京都 · 14 April — 17 April · 3 nights

### Apr 15 (Tue) — Arashiyama & Fushimi Inari

- **08:00** — **Arashiyama Bamboo Grove**: go early to beat crowds; walk to **Tenryu-ji garden**
- **11:00** — **Monkey Park** with panoramic views of Kyoto; lunch at riverside Arashiyama restaurants
- **13:30** — **Sanjusangendo**: breathtaking hall of 1,001 life-size golden Kannon statues — one of the most visually stunning rooms in all of Japan (30–40 min)
- **14:30** — **Fushimi Inari Taisha**: the famous thousands of torii gates; hike as far up the mountain as energy allows
- **18:00** — **Nishiki Market**: Kyoto's famous covered food market — graze on street snacks, pickles, fresh tofu, and local specialties while walking the narrow lane
- **19:30** — Dinner in the area (try kaiseki or yudofu tofu cuisine)

### Apr 16 (Wed) — 🦌 Day Trip to Nara & Uji `DAY TRIP`

- **09:00** — Depart Kyoto Station on JR Nara Line (~45 min, ¥720)
- **10:00** — **Nara Park**: meet the famous **free-roaming sika deer**; buy shika senbei (deer crackers)
- **11:00** — **Todai-ji Temple**: the massive bronze **Great Buddha (Daibutsu)**, one of Japan's most iconic sights; try squeezing through the wooden pillar hole!
- **12:30** — Lunch in Nara; try local kakinoha-zushi or soba
- **14:00** — **Kasuga Taisha**: atmospheric Shinto shrine with hundreds of stone lanterns in a forest setting
- **14:45** — **Nakatanidou**: famous shop where they pound fresh mochi by hand at lightning speed right in front of you — one of Nara's most fun street food moments
- **15:15** — **Naramachi**: historic merchant district; browse craft shops and grab matcha sweets
- **16:00** — Train to **Uji** (~45–50 min from Nara via transfer): famous for green tea and the stunning **Byodo-in Temple** (on the ¥10 coin); grab a matcha soft serve on the way
- **18:00** — Return to Kyoto for dinner (~20 min from Uji)

> 💡 Nara is very walkable — everything is within ~2 km. Great for the whole group. Book Todai-ji tickets online to skip lines. Note: Nara → Uji requires a transfer (no direct train) so allow ~45–50 min, not 30.

| Kyoto → Nara         | Nara → Uji                 | Uji → Kyoto |
| -------------------- | -------------------------- | ----------- |
| JR Nara Line ~45 min | ~45–50 min (with transfer) | ~20 min     |

### Apr 17 (Thu) — Kiyomizu-dera, Kyoto Classics → Osaka `TRANSIT`

- **07:00** — **Kiyomizu-dera Temple**: one of Kyoto's most iconic temples on a wooden hillside platform; magical at dawn before the crowds arrive (opens 06:00)
- **08:00** — Walk **Sannenzaka & Ninenzaka**: beautiful cobblestone streets winding down from the temple; souvenir shops, matcha cafés, traditional townhouses
- **09:30** — **Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)**: iconic gold-leaf temple reflected in a tranquil pond
- **11:00** — **Nijo Castle**: shogun's Kyoto residence; famous "nightingale floors" that squeak when walked on
- **~12:00** — Pick up luggage and take the **Hankyu Line** to Osaka (~45 min, very affordable)
- Check in to Osaka hotel in **Namba / Shinsaibashi** area
- **19:00** — **Dotonbori**: Osaka's electric food & entertainment canal; try **takoyaki** (octopus balls), **okonomiyaki**, and **kushikatsu**

> 💡 **Luggage tip**: Use **takuhaibin** (luggage forwarding) — send your bags from your Kyoto hotel to your Osaka hotel the evening before for ~¥2,000/bag. Ask the hotel front desk on Apr 16. This way you sightsee all morning unburdened and just take the train to Osaka with a day bag.

| Kyoto → Osaka       | Cost          |
| ------------------- | ------------- |
| Hankyu Line ~45 min | ¥410 each way |

## Phase 03 — Osaka & Hiroshima

大阪 · 17 April — 22 April · 5 nights

### Apr 18 (Fri) — Osaka Castle & Shinsekai

- **09:00** — **Osaka Castle**: the castle grounds, museum inside the keep, and the lovely park surrounding it
- **12:00** — **Kuromon Ichiba Market**: Osaka's "Kitchen" — fresh seafood, wagyu skewers, and local snacks
- **14:00** — **Shinsekai district**: retro 1950s-aesthetic neighborhood; try kushikatsu (deep fried skewers — the Osaka invention)
- **18:00** — **Umeda Sky Building**: spectacular open-air observation platform atop two linked towers; stay for sunset

### Apr 19 (Sat) — 🕊️ Day Trip to Hiroshima & Miyajima `DAY TRIP`

- **07:30** — Depart Osaka (Shin-Osaka) on **Shinkansen** to Hiroshima (~1 hr 20 min)
- **09:00–12:00** — **Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park & Museum**: deeply moving; the A-Bomb Dome, the Children's Peace Monument; allow 2–3 hours
- **12:30** — Lunch in Hiroshima; try Hiroshima-style **okonomiyaki** and **oysters** (Hiroshima is Japan's oyster capital)
- **14:00** — Head to **Miyajima Island** (Itsukushima): allow 30–40 min to reach the ferry terminal from the city centre, plus ~10 min ferry ride. Realistic arrival on the island ~14:45–15:00
- Explore the **floating torii gate** of Itsukushima Shrine; roam with free deer; enjoy the island at a relaxed pace
- **18:00** — Ferry back; **Shinkansen back to Osaka**, arrive ~20:00–20:30

> ⚠️ This is a full, emotionally significant day. The Peace Museum is essential but heavy — take breaks. Note: Mt. Misen hike (2–3 hrs with ropeway) is only realistic if you skip something else or start earlier. The shrine area and torii gate alone are well worth the trip. Book Shinkansen in advance for 6 people.

| Shinkansen   | Book ahead       | Miyajima Ferry |

| ~1 hr 20 min | ¥10,520 each way | ~10 min / ¥200 |

- **19:00** — Farewell Kansai dinner: splurge on a proper **kaiseki** or teppanyaki restaurant; celebrate the Kansai leg of the trip

## Phase 04 — Ikebukuro & Tokyo

東京 · 池袋 · 22 April — 27 April · 5 nights

### Apr 23 (Wed) — Shinjuku Gyoen, Meiji Shrine, Harajuku & Shinjuku

- **09:00** — **Shinjuku Gyoen**: one of Tokyo's most beautiful gardens — late cherry blossoms possible mid-April; peaceful and spacious (opens 9:00)
- **10:30** — **Meiji Jingu Shrine**: tranquil forested grounds in the heart of the city
- **12:00** — **Harajuku & Omotesando**: Takeshita Street, the most architecturally impressive shopping boulevard in Japan; lunch here
- **15:00** — **Shinjuku**: Kabukicho entertainment district by day; the Omoide Yokocho (Memory Lane) tiny yakitori alleys
- **20:00** — **Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building**: free observation deck with night views; then ramen in Shinjuku

### Apr 24 (Thu) — Shibuya & Daikanyama

- **09:00** — **Shibuya Sky** (rooftop observation deck) for clear morning views — book the sunset slot with 2-drink option for best experience; then walk the famous scramble crossing from street level
- **10:30** — **Udagawacho backstreets**: Shibuya's hip side-street neighbourhood; plus the enormous **Don Quijote** — the ultimate Japanese discount store for cosmetics, snacks, electronics and souvenirs
- **11:30** — **Daikanyama & Nakameguro**: Tsutaya Books, chic cafés, the canal walk — Tokyo's most relaxed neighbourhoods; lunch here
- **14:30** — **Ebisu Garden Place & Yebisu Beer Museum** (free, fun for the group)
- **18:00** — **Roppongi**: Mori Art Museum (contemporary art with city views) or izakaya evening

### Apr 25 (Fri) — Akihabara, Ueno & Asakusa Revisit

- **09:30** — **Akihabara**: multi-floor electronics shops, anime stores, retro game arcades — allow time to browse
- **12:30** — **Ueno**: Ameyoko market street for souvenirs and street food; lively market atmosphere; lunch here
- **14:00** — **Nezu Shrine**: calm, non-touristy shrine with lovely gardens and a small torii tunnel — a hidden gem between Ueno and Asakusa
- **15:30** — Return to **Asakusa** for any shopping missed; Kaminarimon Gate souvenir photos
- **18:30** — Optional: **teamLab Planets Toyosu** or **teamLab Borderless Azabudai** — immersive digital art (book tickets in advance)
- **20:30** — 🥂 **Farewell dinner for all 6** — splurge on sushi omakase or wagyu yakiniku; last night together as a group
- **22:30** — 🚗 **Akihabara UDX car meetup** (Fridays & Saturdays): informal gathering of JDM and modified cars in the UDX building parking lot — a unique Tokyo late-night bonus for car lovers

### Apr 26 (Sat) — Tsukiji, Last Tokyo — Group Parts Ways

- **08:00** — **Tsukiji Outer Market**: the world-famous seafood market; perfect for a breakfast of fresh sushi, tamagoyaki, and street snacks before the crowds arrive
- **Option A** — **DisneySea Tokyo** (the most beautiful theme park in the world — must book far ahead)
- **Option B — Kawaguchiko & Mt. Fuji**: bus to Kawaguchiko (~1.5 hrs) → **Arakurayama Sengen Park** (the iconic pagoda-with-Fuji photo) → **Mt. Fuji Panoramic Ropeway** → **Oishi Park** lakeside walk → bus back to Tokyo by 17:00. Book bus in advance.
- **Option C** — **Ikebukuro** itself: Sunshine City, the giant Pokémon Center, anime flagship stores; plus last-minute shopping
- **~17:30** — **Azabudai Hills**: Tokyo's brand-new landmark development; free view of **Tokyo Tower from the 60th floor** of Hills House — pair with a walk through **Shiba Park** and **Kokyo Gaien** (Imperial Palace outer gardens)

## Logistics

### Transport Essentials

- **IC Card** (Suica/Pasmo): buy at Haneda on arrival; tap-in everywhere for local trains, buses, even convenience stores
- **Narita ↔ Tokyo**: take the **Keisei Skyliner** (¥2,570) — faster and cheaper than N'EX; runs to Ueno then easy onward to Asakusa/Ikebukuro
- **Kansai Airport → Kyoto**: **Haruka Express** (~¥3,490); book point-to-point tickets at the airport
- **Kyoto → Osaka**: take the **Hankyu Line** from Kyoto-Kawaramachi (¥410) — no booking needed, very frequent
- **Osaka → Kansai Airport**: **Nankai Line** from Namba (~¥920, ~40 min) — simple and cheap
- **Osaka ↔ Hiroshima Shinkansen**: buy individual tickets (¥10,520 each way); book in advance for 6 seats together

### Book in Advance

- **Shinkansen seats** Osaka↔Hiroshima — book early for 6 together
- **Universal Studios Japan** tickets + Express Pass
- **teamLab** timed entry tickets
- **DisneySea** (if chosen) — can sell out months ahead
- **Todai-ji Temple** entry (popular spring dates)
- Popular restaurants: ramen, omakase sushi, teppanyaki for groups
- Hotels that accept **late check-in** for April 22 Ikebukuro arrival (or book a Narita-area hotel for that night)

### Money & Practical Tips

- Japan is still largely **cash-based** — carry yen; 7-Eleven ATMs accept foreign cards
- Get a **pocket WiFi or eSIM** for all 6 people; essential for navigation
- Restaurant reservations: use **Tableall** or **Pocket Concierge** apps for high-end spots
- Google Maps or **HyperDia** app for train navigation
- Convenience stores (konbini) are genuinely amazing — breakfast, lunch, snacks sorted
- Bring an **adapter** (Japan uses Type A / 100V plugs)

### Group of 6 — Special Notes

- Reserve **group seating** on all long-distance trains — Shinkansen has 3+3 layout, fits groups well
- Many taxis fit 4; for 6 people, use **two taxis** or **Go app** (Japanese Uber equivalent)
- Some restaurants have **min. group size limits** — book ahead to confirm
- **Luggage forwarding** (takuhaibin): send bags between hotels for ~¥2,000–3,000/bag — takes 1 day; saves dragging luggage on transit days (Apr 17 Kyoto→Osaka especially). Ask at your hotel the evening before
- **Suica in Apple Wallet**: load directly in the app — no physical card needed; works on trains, buses, and even in convenience stores
- **Shibuya Sky**: book the premium option with 2 drinks; time your slot to catch sunset (~18:30 in April) for the most spectacular views
- April is **Golden Week** end — April 29+ gets very busy. You depart April 26/27, so mostly fine, but expect bigger crowds from April 25
- **April = late cherry blossoms**. Ueno, Maruyama Park (Kyoto), and Osaka Castle Park may still have blossoms early in the trip!
